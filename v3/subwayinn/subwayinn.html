    404: Forget Me Not is a project by Kristina You created as her thesis project at NYU's ITP program in 2023. It is a project about human stories, New York Cities, the effects of gentrification, and preservation of memories. It tells the story of five select NYC stores that have served neighborhoods for countless years but have or on the verge of disappearing. 404 was created as an attempt to create a space for the stories that were created at these locations to live on forever if only on the web.

1932-

SUBWAY INN

1154 SECOND AVE. #8504
NEW YORK, NY

TINA FINEBERG/ THE NEW YORK TIMES

THE SUBWAY INN OPENED A FEW YEARS AFTER PROHIBITION ENDED.

The current owner, Steve S.'s father worked for the original owner, Charlie Ackerman, a man with a reputation as a colorful sourpuss who lived well into his 90s.

OVER THE YEARS, A SPRINKLING OF CELEBRITIES SALTED THE MOSTLY WORKING-CLASS CLIENTELE.

Joe DiMaggio and his wife at the time, Marilyn Monroe, would drop by after a dinner at the nearby restaurant Gino (which closed in 2010). Throughout, beer prices never broke the $6 mark. And the battered banquettes and tiled floor were treated to a dust-to-dust approach to upkeep.

/ MANHATTAN SIDEWAYS

THERE IS NOTHING ARTISANAL, TWEE OR IRONIC ABOUT REAL-DEAL NEW YORK CITY DIVE BARS LIKE THE SUBWAY INN.

Its walls and tables absorbed decades of smoke, beer and heaven knows what else as it offered an after-work haven to construction workers, sales clerks and executives. And like all things that gave this city character, it fell victim to a hot real estate market.

TIMOTHY FADEK/ THE NEW YORK TIMES

UNFORTUNATELY, IN 2014 THE SEVENTY-SEVEN-YEAR-OLD BAR WHERE JOE DIMAGGIO AND MARILYN MONROE USED TO DRINK WAS FORCED TO CLOSE AFTER A LONG BATTLE WITH THE LANDLORDS.

Along with his son, Steven, Marcello Salinas started a petition to save Subway Inn, and while he did not succeed in sparing the bar’s 143 E 60th Street location, in December 2014 they announced that they had found a new home two blocks away.

STEVEN SALINAS, 2014

“This is my dad’s baby. I’m not going to make it look grungy. It wasn’t trashy when Marilyn Monroe came to hang out and it won't be now.”

SALEM KRIEGER PHOTOGRAPHY / SUBWAY INN BAR NYC

IN 2014, SUBWAY INN LOST ITS FIGHT AGAINST ITS LANDLORD, DESPITE A PETITION OF THOUSANDS OF PEOPLE, INCLUDING SINGER TONY BENNETT SUPPORTING THE BAR.

Once the bar closed, it was demolished along with properties from 143 to 161 E. 60th Street; a Chinese-backed company bought the stretch for $300 million.Once it closed, Subway Inn took its neon sign along with the booth shared by Marilyn Monroe and Joe DiMaggio, and moved several blocks east, to 1140 Second Avenue, at East 60th Street.

SINCE THEIR MOVE IN 2014, SUBWAY INN HAS MOVED FOR THE THIRD TIME IN 2023.

Marcelo Salinas, Steven's father died in 2016 but his wife Patricia, who has run the business for 40 years took over the reigns with their son Steven.

SALEM KRIEGER PHOTOGRAPHY / SUBWAY INN BAR NYC

STEVEN SALINAS, 2014

“Everybody’s trying to make more money, People are trying to buy these small businesses out and add to their empire. I don’t think places like this one are going to last too long. We’re a dying breed.”

TIMOTHY FADEK/NEW YORK TIMES
